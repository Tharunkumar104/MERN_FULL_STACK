M S Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, born July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, is widely celebrated as one of cricket’s greatest leaders and finishers. He made his international debut in 2004 and quickly became a sensation with his aggressive batting and sharp wicketkeeping, famously scoring 148 against Pakistan early in his career. Dhoni’s captaincy, beginning in 2007, marked a golden era for Indian cricket: he led India to victory in all three major ICC tournaments—the 2007 T20 World Cup, the 2011 ODI World Cup (where his unbeaten 91 in the final remains iconic), and the 2013 Champions Trophy—making him the only captain to achieve this feat. Under his leadership, India also topped the ICC Test rankings for the first time in 2009, and he became the Indian captain with the most Test wins. Known for his cool temperament and tactical brilliance, Dhoni scored 17,266 international runs, including 16 centuries and 108 half-centuries, and remains the only wicketkeeper to surpass 10,000 ODI runs with an average above 50. As a wicketkeeper, he set records for most dismissals and stumpings in international cricket, with 195 stumpings and 829 dismissals across formats. In the IPL, Dhoni is synonymous with the Chennai Super Kings, leading them to five titles and ten finals in 14 seasons, and holding records for most matches and wins as captain. Even after retiring from international cricket in 2020, Dhoni’s influence endures, both as a player and as a mentor, and he was recently inducted into the ICC Hall of Fame, cementing his legacy as one of the sport’s all-time greats.Dhoni’s rise in cricket was marked by his debut for India in 2004 and his rapid ascent as a dynamic wicketkeeper-batsman. Known for his unorthodox batting style and the famous "helicopter shot," Dhoni became a match-finisher par excellence. His calm and tactical leadership earned him the captaincy of the Indian limited-overs team in 2007 and the Test team in 2008. Under his captaincy, India won all three major ICC trophies: the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy, a unique feat in cricket history. He also led India to the top of the ICC Test rankings for the first time in 2009. Dhoni’s leadership style, often described as "Captain Cool," combined with his sharp cricketing acumen, helped India achieve remarkable success across formats. In the Indian Premier League, he has been the cornerstone of Chennai Super Kings, leading the franchise to five IPL titles and becoming the most successful captain in the tournament’s history.On the personal front, Dhoni married Sakshi Singh Rawat on July 4, 2010, after a few years of courtship. Sakshi, originally from Assam and a hotel management graduate, has been a constant pillar of support, often seen cheering Dhoni during IPL matches. The couple has a daughter named Ziva, born in February 2015, who has garnered a significant following on social media. Dhoni’s dedication to cricket was evident when he chose to continue playing in the 2015 World Cup despite the birth of his daughter, citing national duty as his priority. Beyond cricket, Dhoni is admired for his humility, resilience, and strong family values, which have endeared him to millions. His journey from a small-town boy to a global cricketing legend remains an inspiring story of perseverance, talent, and leadership

Ruturaj Gaikwad

Ruturaj Gaikwad, born on January 31, 1997, in Pune, Maharashtra, has rapidly emerged as one of India’s most promising young cricketers. A technically sound right-handed opener, Gaikwad made his first-class debut for Maharashtra in the 2016-17 Ranji Trophy and soon established himself as a prolific run-scorer in domestic cricket. He has captained Maharashtra in both T20 and List A formats, leading by example with consistent performances, including a remarkable run in the 2021–22 Vijay Hazare Trophy where he scored four centuries and amassed over 600 runs, equalling Virat Kohli’s record for most hundreds in a single season. Gaikwad also made headlines by becoming the first batter to hit seven sixes in an over during the 2022–23 Vijay Hazare Trophy, and he now holds the record for the most centuries in the tournament’s history

Gaikwad’s breakthrough in the Indian Premier League (IPL) came with the Chennai Super Kings (CSK), where he made his debut in 2020 and quickly became a key player. In the 2021 season, he won the Orange Cap for scoring the most runs (635) and was named the Emerging Player of the Year as CSK clinched the title. He continued his strong form in subsequent IPL seasons, helping CSK win their fifth title in 2023. Recognized for his maturity and leadership, Gaikwad was appointed CSK captain in 2024, succeeding MS Dhoni and becoming only the fourth player to lead the franchise. His calm temperament, consistency, and ability to anchor innings have drawn comparisons to his predecessor. On the international stage, Gaikwad debuted for India in T20Is and ODIs after impressive domestic and IPL performances. He has led India’s T20I team to a gold medal at the 2022 Asian Games and scored his maiden international century (123*) in a T20I against Australia in 2023, becoming the first Indian to score a T20I hundred against Australia. Off the field, Gaikwad is known for his humble demeanor and dedication, making him a respected figure among teammates and fans alike. As both a player and a leader, Ruturaj Gaikwad continues to be seen as a vital part of India’s cricketing future, carrying forward a legacy of excellence and composure similar to that of MS Dhoni. Ravindra Jadeja

Ravindra Jadeja, born on December 6, 1988, in Jamnagar, Gujarat, is a celebrated all-rounder in Indian cricket, known for his exceptional skills as a left-arm spinner, lower-order batsman, and agile fielder. He made his international debut in 2009 and quickly established himself as a key player across all formats. Jadeja’s left-arm spin bowling is characterized by its accuracy and ability to extract turn and bounce, making him a formidable opponent on the field. He has been instrumental in many of India’s victories, contributing significantly with both bat and ball.

In the Indian Premier League (IPL), Jadeja has been a standout performer for the Chennai Super Kings (CSK), where he has played since 2012. His all-round capabilities have made him one of the most valuable players in the league, earning him accolades for his match-winning performances. Jadeja’s ability to finish games with the bat, combined with his economical bowling and sharp fielding, has made him a fan favorite.

Dewald Brevis

Dewald Brevis, born on April 29, 2003, in Johannesburg, South Africa, is a rising star in international cricket, known for his explosive batting and exceptional talent. Often compared to AB de Villiers for his aggressive style and versatility, Brevis made headlines during the 2022 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup, where he emerged as the tournament’s leading run-scorer with 506 runs at an average of 84.33, including two centuries and three fifties. His standout performance included a remarkable innings of 178 against Sri Lanka, showcasing his ability to dominate bowlers.

Brevis made his first-class debut for Northerns in South Africa’s domestic circuit in 2021 and quickly established himself as a promising talent. He has also played in various T20 leagues around the world, including the Indian Premier League (IPL), where he was signed by the Mumbai Indians in 2022. His aggressive batting, combined with his ability to play innovative shots, has drawn comparisons to some of the game’s greats.

Ayush Mathre

Ayush Mhatre, born on July 16, 2007, in a Marathi-speaking Samvedi Brahmin family from Walunje, Maharashtra, is a right-handed opening batsman who plays first-class cricket for Mumbai and represents Chennai Super Kings (CSK) in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Growing up in Virar, he began playing cricket at age six and committed seriously to the sport at 15, traveling nearly 80 kilometers daily to train near Wankhede Stadium. Mhatre made his first-class debut in the 2024-25 Irani Cup at 17 and soon scored a maiden century of 176 runs in the Ranji Trophy. He holds the record as the youngest player to score over 150 in men’s List A cricket, surpassing Yashasvi Jaiswal, with a knock of 181 against Nagaland. In IPL 2025, he debuted for CSK at 17 years and 278 days, becoming the franchise’s youngest player, and impressed with a quick 94 off 48 balls against Royal Challengers Bangalore. Known for his aggressive yet technically sound batting and useful off-spin bowling, Mhatre is regarded as a highly promising talent in Indian cricket, combining modern T20 aggression with classical shot-making and temperament that has drawn praise from coaches and experts alike
